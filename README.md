# Nipah-Virus-High-Resolution-Risk-Mapping
Nipah Virus High-Resolution Risk Mapping

Nipah virus is one of the world's deadliest emerging diseases, with 40-75% case fatality rates and pandemic potential recognized by the World Health Organization. Recent outbreaks in Kerala (2018-2025) and the ongoing 2026 West Bengal crisis have exposed critical gaps in our ability to predict where the next spillover will occur.

The Problem: Traditional disease risk maps operate at coarse scales (1-10km) and miss the landscape-level transmission pathways that actually drive outbreaks.

The Solution: This framework provides the first high-resolution (100m) Nipah virus risk assessment that integrates climate suitability, population exposure, and land cover.

This repository contains high-resolution risk mapping for Nipah virus transmission in Bangladesh and South India.

## Features

- **ENM - Median Suitability Layer** (1km resolution)
- **Weighted Risk Surface** (100m resolution)
- Interactive tools for measurement and data queries
- Multiple basemap options (Street, Satellite)

## Layers

### 1. Ecological Niche Model (ENM)
- Resolution: 1km
- Shows ecological suitability for Nipah virus transmission
- Color scale: Gray (low) to Dark Red (high)

### 2. Weighted Risk Surface
- Resolution: 100m
- Combined risk assessment incorporating multiple factors
- Color scale: Light Yellow (low) to Dark Red (high)

## How to Use

1. **[Open the Interactive Map](https://cchfvirus.github.io/Nipah-Virus-High-Resolution-Risk-Mapping/)**
2. Select a layer from the left panel
3. Click on the map to view risk values at specific locations
4. Right-click to copy coordinates
5. Use drawing tools to measure distances and areas
